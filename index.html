ABOUT ME

The Journey of an Ambitious Game Developer/Designer

— A quick read —
Subject: summary

The fall.

Becoming a successful freelance web developer is something I will always be proud of. Although it was never the intent of becoming one in the first place, I was rather a hobby that I found creativity in. After graduating from high school, I had my eyes set on studying in the field of medicine for a future occupation. During my natural science studies, I had always felt a part of me was slowly drifting away, and that was my creative nature. I strived on my inner mission of becoming someone who can help the many, one of my many cliche reasons for wanting to study medicine, but I was determined to do so. In the fall of 2021, I made – in what I consider to be – the biggest interpersonal and academic mistake in my life to date: transferring to the University of California, Davis to study Human Biology. I don’t hold anything against what I had learned during my year at UC Davis, as we will always find valuable lessons and knowledge from errors. Studying Human Biology, I learned a lot about the human physically – of course, but what also makes the human such as the reasons behind the decisions we make. However, unfazed by my sheer determination of a strong self-sacrifice mentality, I failed to recognise that I was learning more about others and understanding who I am less. Towards the end of my winter quarter, I concluded that what I was doing was doable by a robot, in a sense, I have lost all reasonable drive to continue on that path.

The Mission.

Upon weeks of reflection and planning, it turned out that what I really wanted to do was, to simply put it, make games in which people can enjoy. To me, games had a large impact on who I am today. They were the escape in which I had from lesser favourable experiences of my youth. They are the foundation of my creativity in which I have been able to express and have others enjoy. As a web developer and graphic designer, I have unknowingly been doing a fraction of what I have always wanted to do. Nonetheless, the works I have done to express myself have been the stepping stones which have concluded to be the foundations of my future goal: to become a game developer and designer.
